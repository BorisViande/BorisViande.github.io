This model is a work in progress.

 

The aim of the Capella model is to support the MBSE effort in the conception of the Lunar Integrated Shelter for Exploration (LISE). It is meant as a way to first establish the operational context of the system of interest (SOI) and to capture its mission concept and objectives.

This work is done partially in parallel with SpartanSpace which is in charge of the mission analysis as well as the creation of the preliminary technical spécifications of LISE.

The role of the model is in part to serve as a support to ellicit the operational needs of the SOI but also as a way to capture and communicate information about the system. It shall help to ensure consistency and completeness of the system definition.

 

The entry to the model should be the Operational Architecture of LISE which displays the context in which is inscribed the SOI.

LISE is a lunar deployable habitat. It is envisionned as a supporting system which could provide additionnal capabilities for astronauts in the context of NASA's Artemis programme.

 

Transitionning from the context to the acutal SOI, the SOI Balck-box architecture presents the high-level system functions which encompass the functional needs of the system.
